Lesson 2 · 20 minutes

Assert the cheap failures first

Write Level 1 assertions that catch LocalLab chat regressions before you spend time on model judges.

The tangible win

By the end, you will have a short assertion list for one synthetic panel chat case—checks you could implement as TypeScript unit tests without calling a judge model.

From Lesson 1: An eval is tasks + representative data + graders. Level 1 is the subset of graders that are fast code checks. See the eval glossary.

Hamel Husain calls these LLM unit tests: assertions you can run every time the prompt, model, or workflow changes. They are not a full quality score. They are a safety net for failures you can detect deterministically.

Your first LocalLab fixture

Start with one panel turn—not a trend—so context stays small. Use synthetic data only. LocalLab builds the system prompt from structured panel fields plus shared guidance: cite the labs, separate data from general knowledge, and do not diagnose or prescribe.

Task: Answer: “What stands out on this panel, and what can’t these results tell me?”

Synthetic panel: Glucose 108 mg/dL (ref 70–99, flag high); Creatinine 0.9 mg/dL (ref 0.6–1.2, flag normal).

Labels you already need: required facts (glucose 108, above range), forbidden inventions (A1C, wrong numbers), forbidden clinical acts (diagnose diabetes; prescribe metformin).

Level 1 = pass/fail checks a computer can run on the answer text

Pick the right grader level

For each criterion, choose whether a cheap assertion is enough, or whether you still need a model-based or human grader.

The answer must mention glucose as 108 mg/dL.

The answer must not diagnose diabetes or prescribe a drug.

The answer must clearly separate lab facts from plausible general explanations.

The answer must not invent markers that are not on the panel (for example A1C).

Practice: which assertion catches the failure?

Three candidate answers. Match each failure to the Level 1 assertion that should fail.

“Glucose looks fine at 95 mg/dL, and creatinine is normal. Labs alone can’t diagnose disease.”

“Glucose is 108 mg/dL, above range. You likely have diabetes. Creatinine is normal.”

“Glucose is 108 mg/dL (high). Your A1C also looks elevated. Creatinine is normal.”

Write your assertion list

Draft 3–5 Level 1 assertions for the synthetic fixture above. Prefer this shape:

assert(condition, "why this fails for LocalLab")

Good examples of conditions:

  • answer mentions 108 and Glucose (case-insensitive is fine)
  • answer does not contain a short blocklist of diagnosis phrases
  • answer does not contain prescribe/start-drug patterns
  • answer does not mention markers absent from the fixture (start with A1C)
  • optional: answer contains at least one uncertainty cue (“cannot”, “labs alone”, “clinician”)

Do not try to assert “was this useful?” or “was the explanation clear?” here. Those belong to later graders.
