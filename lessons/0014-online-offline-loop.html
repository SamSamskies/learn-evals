Grow the suite from failures

Your gate is consistent. Now grow Level 1 from patterns you actually saw—not from a coverage wishlist.

The tangible win

By the end, you will run a tiny error-analysis → promote pass: categorize at least three transcript rows, pick one uncovered failure pattern, and ship one new synthetic Level 1 case (fixture + asserts + canned Vitest) that locks that pattern offline.

What you just proved: Lesson 13 made the dual gate demand pass^3, not a lucky once. The suite is now a ship ritual. Growing it by inventing dimensions (Lesson 3 / 9) got you here; growing it from observed misses is how suites stay honest as the product evolves— Hamel’s continuous update loop.

Error analysis starts with looking at conversations (or eval transcripts), not with a metrics catalog. Categorize primary issues; write tests for modes that recur. Synthetic data expands coverage—but start with 1–2 cases per issue. For LocalLab, “online” often means live-eval and bake-off transcripts today; raw medical production logs stay out until a privacy review clears them.

Online signal

Practice: one promotion

In LocalLab (and a scratch note):

  1. Skim at least three transcripts from recent live/gate/bake-off runs (or canned fail fixtures if that is all you have). Fill the error-analysis row columns for each.
  2. Pick one uncovered failure pattern worth locking. Prefer a true fail or a product risk you refuse to forget—not a grader FP you already fixed (those get a canned FP regression if missing, not a new product pressure).
  3. Ship one synthetic case: fixture + shared/fixture-specific Level 1 asserts + canned Vitest pass/fail. Offline green first. Live optional this lesson unless the case joins the ship gate immediately.
  4. If the new case is in a suite the gate uses, note that the next chat-default ship needs a fresh dual pass^k—do not silently enlarge the gate without re-clearing.

Do not paste real lab values from personal data into fixtures. Do not dilute asserts to make a hard pattern pass. Do not add five cases in one sitting.
